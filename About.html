My name is Emilija Pukevičiūtė, and I was born in Klaipėda, Lithuania. Since I was a little girl, I believed that in the future, I would be working in a field that combines creativity and communication.
In a way, I was not wrong, as I am currently studying contemporary communications at LCC International University and learning about various things, from writing articles to meeting diverse people from all around the world.
This place taught me how to be more open-minded and express myself and my work, and for that, I am very grateful.

Writing is one of the coping mechanisms that helps me resolve my problems, control my emotions, and feel free when expressing myself.
When I was 18 years old, I started to write poems, but it seemed strange that I had ideas only when I was sad or overwhelmed.
Most of my poems consist of themes such as war, loneliness, freedom, fakeness, and many more.
After writing poems for one year, I realized that I feel more relaxed. I learned how to control my emotions because every time I feel like I am about to "explode," I take a pen and write what comes to mind.
I strongly encourage people to take a pen and write their thoughts and feelings. This method is like therapy, only it is free, and it allows you to express what is in your heart.
