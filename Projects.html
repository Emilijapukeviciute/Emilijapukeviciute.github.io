February 24

Find me in this silence, so exhausting and deep,
Help me know myself in the moonlight I keep.
I’m lost in despair, my thoughts a wild chase,
Which I can’t escape, they won’t leave a trace.

Agony surrounds me, a firm, tight embrace,
Bound by time’s grip, I can't find my place.
Help me bear this burden, heavy and vast,
For in your hands, my breath is confined fast.

The night sky is painted in hues of deep red,
I’m alone among many, and fear fills my head.
I run and I rush, while cobwebs ensnare,
My soul’s just as mortal as hope in the air.

My goal is to win, yet that’s not enough,
I long to break free, to be bold and tough.
When that day comes, and I’m not by your side,
Please remember my hope, as it floats with the tide.

Childhood

To extinguish the flames that burn in the distance is tough,
Confessing to you all the burdens I've had is quite rough.
Forgiving the one who loves me feels far too painful,
I long to escape from this home that's so shameful.

Winding scars tell the tale of my childhood days,
Empty courtyards reflect what motherhood conveys.
Dazed by the moss, they embark on their quest,
While I stand, hungry, beneath this roof's crest.

Rain fills the earth with a void it can't bear,
Melted snow brightens the joys of youth's air.
Children, so happy, will run through the dew,
Full yards will remind me of dreams untrue.

I stand on bricks that are not yet in place,
Always waiting for you, like the morning’s embrace.
I rush by the stream, seeking solace and grace,
I pause, knowing they’ll never return to this space.

The mask

Faceless wanderers drift through the night,
Living in shadows, hidden from sight.
Caught in the webs of their pointless despair,
Alone in the battle of feelings laid bare.

Souls that are sinking in sorrowful plight,
Remind me of rainbows that fade from the light.
A landscape of bleakness, in black and in white,
They strive to release you from this endless fight.

Bind me up tightly, let shadows not flee,
Bound bodies are trapped and can’t move to be free.
Sleepless nights blend with days filled with pain,
True bodies are fading, ensnared by the chain.

Reality mingles with illusion's embrace,
Family and friends have lost all their grace.
Miracles won’t change what’s already infected,
To have a stranger feels all too accepted.

Light

I saw a bright light hidden deep in the wood,
I’ll meet you there in the dream where I stood.
I want to end this journey and rescue your heart,
And rest in peace, as you promised from the start.

I no longer wish to stay where darkness prevails,
I just want to understand where all of time trails.
Beneath the rain clouds, I’ll find my way home,
But sadly, I know that you won’t be there to roam.

Your voice will echo like a strong ocean wave,
In my eyes, I’ll see storms of the memories I save.
Birds will entice me to fly away with you,
Yet my thoughts will hold back, unsure of what’s true.

We’ll meet in winter when the lilies will fade,
I’ll run to you, like a soldier betrayed.
With pain, I’ll find healing, though scars will still show,
For the hurt I have carried has long since grown.

Mother

I’ll bring you a bouquet of wildflowers fair,
Embracing you tightly, my heart feels the wear.
I’ll tell you a dream of a childhood so bright,
And only you’ll remind me what mothering's light.

You’ll heal all the scars that life’s journey has left,
You’ll understand how without you, I’m bereft.
I just want to see you, to hold you so near,
Beneath the starry sky, share tales without fear.

I’ll cradle your silver hair close to my chest,
I’ll gather your tears that from cheeks have expressed.
Together we’ll laugh at life’s trials and pains,
While my heart will recall all the joy that remains.

Stronghold

They fiercely gather the courage to oppose
Urging lies, stirring our holy homes -
Without them, our gathered calling fades,
For a battle without arms is life without aims.

I call upon the nation, I call upon you!
Has the painful end now come to be?
To triumph over evil is my honest duty;
To surrender to rejection, I shall say ‘no’ truly.

Desire and fear, the start of my stronghold.
Let suffering cause suffering to your body, be bold!
I, as a falcon, will soar in the sky’s embrace;
I will find you, and together, we will glow at dusk’s grace.

The foundations of my land trampled red.
In the distance, women’s cries plead,
Children’s tiny footsteps are lost in the unknown.
It is your fault for letting death’s seed be sown.

Clasping hands, we won’t retreat to the sea,
We will shout victory and march into the battle.
Our blood, the imprints leading to the future’s door;
Perhaps tomorrow, we will rise to the final response.
